# Fernlight — Marketing Agency Plan

*A working playbook for what we do, who we serve, how we land clients, and how we grow. Written so anyone joining the team can get the full picture in one read.*

---

## 1. What Fernlight Is

Fernlight is a done-for-you marketing agency for local businesses. We do two things, and we do them together:

1. **Build websites** — modern, fast, conversion-focused sites for businesses that have a weak site or none at all.
2. **Run ads** — paid advertising campaigns (Meta and Google) that drive real leads and customers to those businesses.

The combination is the whole point. A website with no traffic is a brochure nobody reads. Ads pointing at a bad website burn money. We deliver both halves so the client gets an actual lead-generation machine — not an asset that just sits there.

**Who it's for:** Local businesses in the San Diego area that are clearly underinvested online (outdated or missing websites, little to no paid advertising) but operate in **higher-ticket verticals** where one new customer is worth a lot. We're starting with **real estate** — for an agent, one closed deal is worth thousands in commission, so the math on paying for leads is easy for them to say yes to.

**The expansion path:** Win San Diego real estate first → widen to adjacent local verticals (home services, med spas, legal, etc.) → then expand geographically beyond SD.

## 2. Who We Serve (and Why We Niche Down First)

**Ideal first client:**
- San Diego–area real estate agent or small brokerage
- Weak or no website, little or no paid advertising
- Actively wants more leads (a working professional, not a hobbyist)
- Can afford a monthly retainer plus ad spend

**Why we start narrow instead of "any business":**
- **One message, one funnel, repeatable.** We're not reinventing the pitch every call.
- **Referrals compound.** Agents know other agents. A tight vertical turns one happy client into a pipeline.
- **Case studies transfer.** A win for one agent directly sells the next one.
- **We get fast.** Doing the same kind of build and the same kind of ad campaign repeatedly makes delivery quicker and cleaner.

Spreading across ten industries at once means ten times the messaging, ten funnels, and no compounding. We go deep before we go wide.

## 3. What We Sell (Offer & Pricing)

Two core deliverables, packaged so they're easy to say yes to.

### A. Website build *(one-time)*
- Modern, mobile-first, fast, built to convert — clear call-to-action, lead capture, follow-up wired in from day one.
- **Suggested range: $750–$2,500** depending on scope.
- Can be discounted for the first 1–2 clients to build case studies (but **not free** — see below).

### B. Ad management *(monthly retainer + client-funded ad spend)*
- We build, launch, and optimize Meta/Google campaigns and manage the lead flow.
- **Suggested: flat $500–$1,500/mo management, or 15–20% of ad spend — whichever is higher.**
- **The client pays the actual ad spend directly** to Meta/Google, so it's not our cost.
- Includes automated lead follow-up (this is where GoHighLevel comes in — instant text-back, missed-call recovery, nurture sequences).

### The packaged offer *(my recommendation)*
> **"The Lead Engine"** — We build your site, launch your ads, and wire up automated follow-up so every new lead gets contacted in under a minute. One setup fee to get it live, then a flat monthly to keep it running and optimized.

Packaging it as one product (instead of "à la carte website + ads") makes it simpler to sell and simpler to deliver.

### Pricing the first client
Offer a **founder rate** (discounted) to land the first one or two clients and earn case studies — but keep it **paid**. A paying client, even at a discount, takes the work seriously and proves real demand. A free client proves nothing and trains you to give the work away. Raise prices the moment you have one result to point to.

## 4. How We Get Clients (The Outreach Engine)

This is our **client-acquisition system** — completely separate from what we deliver. It's deliberately cheap so we can bootstrap with almost no money.

### The list
- ~1,000 San Diego–area businesses scraped into a CSV: `business_name`, `phone`, `email`, `website`, `industry` — sorted by industry, prioritizing weak/no web presence.
- We work **one segment at a time**, starting with real estate.

### Primary channel: conversational SMS (via GoHighLevel)
- We send from a **local 619 number** — it reads as a real San Diego local, which gets far higher reply rates than a toll-free line. (An 800 number screams "marketing blast.")
- The approach is **conversational, not a broadcast.** The first text only has to earn a reply; we take it from there to a booked call.
- **Requires A2P 10DLC registration before any texts send** (see Section 7). Real gate — budget a few days for approval.

### The SMS sequence

**1. Opener** *(the only message that fires automatically)*
> Hey, is this {{business_name}}?
*(append the opt-out line — e.g. "...reply STOP to opt out")*

Short on purpose — short texts get the highest reply rate, and "is this you?" almost always pulls a "yeah, who's this?"

**2. After they reply — intro + qualify**
> Yeah hey, I'm Gio — based here in SD. Quick one: are you running your own marketing/lead gen, or do you have someone on it?

**3. The pivot — the pitch**
> Gotcha. So I help San Diego real estate offices pull in more inbound leads — sharper site + automated follow-up so you're not chasing people by hand. Took a look at your setup and there's a couple easy wins. Want me to send 'em over, or easier to hop on a quick 10-min call?

**4. Book the call**
> Perfect — here's my calendar, grab whatever slot works: {{link}}

**No-reply bump** *(after 24h, once only)*
> Hey {{business_name}}, still the right number? Not a bot, promise — just had a quick idea for you.

**Clean exit on a no** *(protects the number)*
> All good, appreciate the reply. Around if it's ever useful — Gio

**How it runs in GHL:** Only message 1 goes out from the campaign. Replies land in the Conversations inbox and we handle 2–4 live (saved as snippets for speed). The no-reply bump can be an automated wait-24h → send step.

### Complementary channels *(my suggestions)*
- **Cold email** — we already have emails in the list. No A2P gate, easy compliance (identify sender, real opt-out), and it runs in parallel to multiply reach.
- **Referrals** — build a referral ask into delivery from day one. In a tight vertical like real estate, this becomes the cheapest and best channel fast.
- **Local presence** — we're SD-based; in-person drop-ins and local networking punch above their weight for high-ticket local clients.

## 7. Compliance & Risk *(read this — no surprises)*

Being straight so we go in with eyes open.

- **A2P 10DLC is mandatory** to send business SMS to US numbers. Brand + campaign registration, ~3–7 day approval, and it requires real legal info (an EIN, or sole-proprietor registration under a legal name + US address + a phone OTP). **No texts deliver until it clears.**
- **Consent is the real issue.** Cold outreach to a scraped list means recipients never opted in. That creates **TCPA exposure** (statutory damages per text) and means the carrier consent question can't be honestly answered "yes." This is **legally grey territory** — we operate it informed, not blind.
- **Number health.** Spam complaints degrade a number fast; carriers suspend senders whose opt-out rate crosses ~3%. The conversational opener and clean exits keep complaints low, but practically, **cold-outreach numbers are semi-disposable** — expect to rotate them, and **never run the core business identity through the blasting number.**
- **Guardrails we always keep:** identify ourselves, include an opt-out, exit gracefully on a no, and never use the prospecting number for anything we can't afford to lose.
- **The clean long-term path:** as we grow, the lowest-risk acquisition is inbound + referrals + opt-in lead magnets, which sidestep all of the above. **SMS is the scrappy bootstrap, not the forever plan.**

## 9. 30-Day Launch Plan — to First Paying Client

Tight and milestone-driven. The goal of month one is **one paying client** — not a big build-out.

**Week 1 — Setup**
- Pull the real-estate segment out of the 1,000-lead list
- Buy the 619 number ✅ *(done)* → submit A2P 10DLC registration
- Finalize the SMS sequence, build the GHL campaign + booking calendar
- Build one strong sample/portfolio site to show on calls

**Week 2 — Launch outreach**
- A2P approved → start texting the first ~200–300 real-estate leads
- Work replies live, book discovery calls
- Track: reply rate, call-book rate

**Week 3 — Close**
- Run the discovery calls, present the Lead Engine offer
- Goal: **close 1 founder-rate client**
- Kick off their website build

**Week 4 — Deliver + prove**
- Ship the first client's site, launch their ads
- Capture early results → **first case study**
- Use it to sharpen the pitch and raise the price for client #2

**Success for month 1:** one paying client live and one case study in progress. That's validation. Everything scales from a proven, repeatable win — so we **resist adding verticals or channels until this loop works once.**
